Additional experiments were undertaken to establish the reaction mechanism; it was found that the presence of TEMPO [(2,2,6,6-tetramethylpiperidin-1-yl)oxyl] or oxygen completely inhibits the reaction. The authors assumed that DMSO is deprotonated on treatment with Bu t OK to give the anion. This anion gives off the electron to another DMSO molecule to generate radical A. Hydrogen abstraction from the substrate affords a-aminoalkyl radical B. The subsequent intramolecular radical cyclization gives intermediate C. There are pathways for direct introduction of the amine group into the ortho-position relative to the alkynyl group in arylacetylene molecules, which can be catalyzed, in particular, by transition metal compounds.

A versatile and efficient method for the synthesis of diverse N-aminoindoles is based on Pd-catalyzed domino reactions of easily accessible 2-halophenylacetylenes with simple N,N-disubstituted hydrazines. 129 The reactions proceed in good to high yields, take several hours under mild conditions and are applicable to substrates with various functional groups (Scheme 96).

In another Pd-catalyzed process for introduction of the amino group into the aromatic ring, nitrogen comes from tert-butylsulfinamide (84), 130 which behaves as an equivalent of ammonia in the Pd-catalyzed amination of aryl bromides and chlorides. The Pd(OAc)2-Xantphos [4,5-bis(diphenylphosphino)-9,9-dimethylxanthene] system served as the catalyst in both steps.
