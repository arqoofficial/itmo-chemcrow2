## 2.5. Reissert indole synthesis

The first step of the classical Reissert indole synthesis comprises the condensation of o-nitrotoluene with diethyl oxalate to give ethyl o-nitrophenylpyruvic acid (Scheme 37). 72 The subsequent reduction of the nitro group leads to spontaneous cyclization giving esters of indole-2-carboxylic acid.

A modern version of the Reissert synthesis includes the catalytic hydrogenation in a continuous-flow reactor (H-Cube continuous-flow hydrogenation reactor) (Scheme 38, the yields are given according to the HPLC data with UV detection). 73 The best results were attained in the synthesis of 2-ethoxycarbonylindole (93% yield) at a hydrogen pressure of 1 bar in an ethyl acetate-ethanol mixture (1:1) at a flow rate of 1 ml min 71 and at 20 8 C.

Recently, an unusual domino reaction has been proposed in which the same α-carbon atom of furan reacts, first, as a nucleophile and then as an electrophile. In the presence of acids, N-tosylfurfurylamine (66) reacts with 2-(tosylamino)benzyl alcohol giving rise to 2-(acylvinyl)indoles 67a-n (Scheme 58, Table 6). 94 The reaction proceeds as the Friedel-Crafts alkylation of furan position 2 followed by acid-catalyzed intramolecular attack by the ortho-amino group on the same atom.

The process optimization is mainly related to the choice of the acid. Three acidic agents, namely, AcOH-HCl (method a), CF3CO2H (method b) and CuSO4. 5 H2O (method c), were tested with a broad range of substrates.

Method a resulted in the lowest yields of indoles 67e, f, l. The use of primary benzyl alcohol gave a moderate yield of indole 67n. This can be attributed to lower stability of primary carbocations, which leads to oligomerization and, as a consequence, to resinification of the reaction mixture. The presence of electron-withdrawing substituents in the benzene ring in the para-position relative to the amino group substantially decreases the yields of indoles (products 67i, j).

The replacement of the leaving tosylamino group by the phthalimide group (furan 68) permits the synthesis of indoles 69 of another type owing to the amphiphilic nature of the C(2) atom (Scheme 59).
