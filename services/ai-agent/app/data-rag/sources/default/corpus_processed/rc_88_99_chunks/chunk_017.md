## 8.2. C 7 Nbond formation

Most often, the C 7 N bond formation in the indole synthesis occurs with the participation of anilines with an appropriate functional group in the ortho -position to the amino group. An example of such groups is vinyl, which undergoes the C 7 H amination followed by oxidation to give a pyrrole ring.

Efficient intramolecular C 7 H amination in N -tosyl2-alkenylanilines requiring no transition metal-based catalysts and using DDQ as the oxidant was utilized for the synthesis of a broad range of substituted indoles (Scheme 147). 206 The mechanism of this process includes the generation of a radical cation via single-electron transfer (SET) and migration steps in the intermediate phenonium ion. Apart from the absence of metal catalysts, benefits of the method include experimental simplicity and large diversity of suitable substrates.
