## 2.2. Nenitzescu reaction

The classical Nenitzescu synthesis of 5-hydroxyindoles comprises the condensation of p-benzoquinones with aminocrotonates (Scheme 22). 49

This method still has not lost its significance, as indicated by the continuously appearing publications reporting its modification. In 2010, it was demonstrated that this method is applicable for the industrial production of 5-hydroxy-2-methyl-1H-indole-3-carboxylic acid (in 65 kg quantity) and 5-hydroxy-2-methyl-1H-indole (in 20 kg quantity). 50 The replacement of p-benzoquinone by quinoneimines 32, which are readily obtained by the oxidation of the appropriate p-alkoxyanilines, and the subsequent condensation with hexafluoroacetylacetone (in the Nenitzescu synthesis, aminocrotonates are used) afford o-aminobenzyl diketone derivatives 33, which readily undergo cyclization to 3-(trifluoroacetyl)indoles (Scheme 23, Table 2). 51 Thus, in this case, quinone imine serves as the source of indole nitrogen and phenyliodine(III) diacetate acts as the oxidant, and elimination of a water molecule is induced by trifluoroacetic acid.

This method has some substrate limitations. Thus it follows from Table 2 that in the presence of electron-withdrawing substituents in the benzene ring, indoles are rarely formed. An important practical application of the Nenitzescu reaction is the synthesis of tert-butyl 2-methyl-4-(trifluoromethyl)-1H-indole-3-carboxylate, a key intermediate in the synthesis of androgen receptor modulators. A convenient method has been developed for the synthesis of this indole from 4-amino-3-(trifluoromethyl)phenol and tert-butyl acetoacetate (Scheme 24). In 1968, Litell and Allen 52 showed that the Nenitzescu reaction of 2-trifluoromethyl-1,4-benzoquinone with 3-aminocrotonates leads to the selective formation of 4-substituted 5-hydroxyindoles. The regioselectivity is caused by the strong inductive effect of the trifluoromethyl group. Hence, the enamine addition occurs selectively at position 3 of benzoquinone.

Unlike the synthesis of 5-hydroxyindoles, the preparation of 6-hydroxyindoles is faced with some obstacles. Meanwhile, compounds of this class have found practical use as inhibitors of target proteins, hair colourant, cannabinoid receptor agonists (JWH-200, 34a) and fatty acid amide hydrolase (FAAH) inhibitors (34b); their fragments are encountered in indole alkaloid molecules (notoamide J, 34c). 53-56

The Nenitzescu synthesis is usually employed to prepare 5-hydroxyindole derivatives by the reaction of 1,4-benzoquinones with aminocrotonates. The Nenitzescu reaction with reversed regioselectivity resulting in 6-hydroxyindoles is encountered very rarely. This reaction, which depends on the type of substituent in the aminoacrylate, was discovered by Grinev and co-workers, 57 who replaced the N-alkyl derivatives by N-aryl derivatives (Scheme 25).
