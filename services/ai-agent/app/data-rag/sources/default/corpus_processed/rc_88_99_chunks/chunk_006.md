An interesting way of using a hydrogenated isoxazole analogue has been described. 99 First, attention is attracted by the preparation of substrates: in the presence of O2 and IPrCuCl [IPr is 1,3-bis(2,6-diisopropylphenyl)imidazol-2ylidene] (5 mol.%), [3+2]-cycloaddition of N-hydroxyallenylamines to nitrosobenzenes results in the desired 5-hydroxyisoxazolidines. Heating of this reaction mixture with DBU in toluene affords functionalized indoles (Scheme 67).

The mechanism of indole formation shown in Scheme 68 implies a key role of ketene imine intermediates: DBU promotes opening of the initial ketal to give intermediate A, which then undergoes catalytic rearrangement to the O-bonded isomer C via the intermediate formation of ketene imine species B. Unlike N-isomer A, intermediate C

actively undergoes the 3,3-rearrangement to give 2-aryl3-oxoamides D and E, which are finally converted to indoles.

An example of conversion of fused thiophene derivatives to indoles has been reported. The sulfone group in dibenzothiophene dioxides (e.g., compound 79), which are easily prepared by oxidation of dibenzothiophenes, undergoes, first, intermolecular and then intramolecular nucleophilic aromatic substitution with anilines, thus yielding carbazoles (Scheme 69). 100 This transformation of dibenzothiophenes to carbazoles does not require catalysis by heavy metals.

It is worth mentioning that the use of NaHMDS, instead of KHMDS, considerably decreases the product yield (for Rn = 4-Me, from 94% to 50%). The reaction proceeds equally smoothly for anilines with different types of substituents in the benzene ring and gives carbazoles in high yields. The reaction tolerates the vinyl group, although the corresponding carbazole is formed in a moderate yield. The TIPS group is partly hydrolyzed under the reaction conditions, resulting in a mixture of carbazoles with Rn = TIPSO and OH in 1:2.6 ratio. Unfortunately, unprotected p-aminophenol reacts to a low yield (26%).

Among the five-membered heterocycles involved in the transformations to indoles, a special, rather peculiar, role is played by triazole derivatives (Scheme 71). 101 Formerly, the preparation of thiadiazine derivatives of type 81 from 4-amino-3-sulfanyl-4H-triazole (80) and subsequent trans-

formation with C7S (Ref. 102) and N7N (Ref. 103) bond cleavage have been reported. While analyzing reactions of this type, the authors arrived to the conclusion that the C7S bond cleavage can be used to synthesize 2-arylindoles.

The developed method serves as an example of indole synthesis based on a heterocyclic matrix. 4-Amino-3-sulfanyltriazole 80 was used as the matrix that was later separated during the workup of the reaction mixture. The reaction can be implemented as a one-pot procedure. Although the product yields are low, this method has benefits such as ready accessibility of the starting compounds and a simple experimental procedure.
