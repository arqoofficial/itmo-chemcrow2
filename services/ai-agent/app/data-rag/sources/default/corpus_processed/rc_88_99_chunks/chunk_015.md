The putative mechanism of the process is summarized in Scheme 106. The cross-coupling affords N,N-dialkyl-2-(1-alkynyl)aniline A. Aryl iodide is added after completion of the cross-coupling. The oxidative addition of aryl iodide to Pd0 yields the electrophilic intermediate ArPdI, which activates the triple bond of structure A via coordination, thus giving palladium p-complex B. The latter immediately undergoes the intramolecular transamination according to the 5endo-dig-cyclization pattern to afford indolium intermediate C, which eliminates the methyl group as a result of SN2 substitution by the in situ generated iodide anion, thus yielding indole-containing PdII intermediate D.

2,3-Disubstituted indole is generated upon the reductive elimination.

A convenient one-pot regioselective synthesis of 2,3-disubstituted indoles from 2-bromoanilines is based on successive Pd-catalyzed Sonogashira cross-coupling with alkylacetylenes, amidopalladation and reductive elimination. 145

A rhodium-catalyzed introduction of an alkynyl group into the ortho-position of phenylhydrazines accompanied by in situ indole formation has been reported. 146

In an interesting synthetic route to 2-haloindole-3-carboxylic acids, instead of the ethynyl group, the dihalovinyl group was employed as the ethynyl group precursor (Scheme 107). 147 The optimal conditions of synthesis of 2-halo-3-carboxyindoles from 2-(2,2-dihalovinyl)anilines include heating of the substrates in DMSO at 120 8C in the presence of caesium carbonate for several minutes. However, the reaction can successfully proceed over a broad temperature range in aqueous media and can be conducted using various combinations of a polar solvent and a carbonate base; the only limiting factor is the substrate solubility. Substituents present in 2-(2,2-dihalovinyl)anilines are, most often, tolerant to the reaction conditions and the product yields are usually high. The reaction mechanism includes the initial formation of the alkynyl bromide intermediate followed by cyclization and addition of carbon dioxide. Due to the nature of the intermediate, we attributed the cited publication to the Section devoted to the use of o-alkynylanilines in indole synthesis.
