The replacement of the leaving tosylamino group by the phthalimide group (furan 68) permits the synthesis of indoles 69 of another type owing to the amphiphilic nature of the C(2) atom (Scheme 59).

Indoles of both types (67a and 69a-c) are formed upon the reaction of N-furfurylbenzamides (Scheme 60). 94

The mechanism of formation of these indoles is depicted in Scheme 61. The results can be interpreted in terms of the competitive protonation of intermediate 70 either at the amide group or at the furan C(5) atom. The protonation at the amide group (pathway a) substantially increases its nucleofugality (cation A), which leads to the formation of cation B upon amide elimination. The nucleophilic attack by the 2-tosylamino group on the furan C(2) atom followed by opening of the spiro ring in intermediate C generates indole 67. The protonation of the C(5) atom of furan (pathway b) affords cation D capable of generating spiro intermediate E, which is converted to intermediate F via tautomerization. It is noteworthy that in this case, too, the C(2) atom of furan demonstrates a dual reactivity, acting as a nucleophile in the step towards intermediate 70 and as an electrophile as the latter reacts with the 2-tosylamino group to yield intermediate E.

A simple and efficient synthesis of indoles 67 is based on thermolysis of 2-(2-azidobenzyl)furans with attack by the resulting nitrene on the ipso-position of the furan moiety (Scheme 62). 95 The cyclization is accompanied by furan ring opening and gives high yields of indoles with 2-acetylvinyl substituent at C(2), capable of further transformations.

Apparently, indole 67 is formed upon the electrophilic attack by the nitrene, generated via azide thermolysis, on furan position 2 to give spiro-zwitter-ion A (Scheme 63; shown for the substrate with R1 = H, R2 = Ph, R3 = Me). 95 Then the furan ring is opened, giving rise to 4-(3-phenyl-3H-indol-2-yl)but-3-en-2-one 71 and aromatization of the latter yields indole (Z)-67a, which isomerizes to the final indole (E)-67a.

A convenient method for the synthesis of 3,4-disubstituted 5-hydroxyindoles, which are used in synthetic and biological studies, consists in the following one-pot cascade of reactions: microwave-initiated intramolecular [4+2] cycloaddition of the alkynol group to furan, fragmentation and aromatization of the adduct and removal of the Boc protecting group. 96 The yields of indoles range from 15% to 74%; the presence of aromatic substituents provides higher yields. In earlier studies, 97 a sequence of reactions was developed, in which the 1,2-addition of lithiated derivative 72 to enone 73 affords allylic alcohol 74; microwave irradiation of this product gives tricyclic Diels-Alder adduct A; successive elimination of a water molecule and the protecting group gives indoles 75 via intermediates B and C (Scheme 64). If alkynone is taken instead of enone, the reaction follows a different pathway: cleavage of the oxygen bridge in adduct A affords structure D, aromatization of which in an early step yields phenol E. Elimination of one water molecule from E with simultaneous deprotection furnishes the target indole 76.
