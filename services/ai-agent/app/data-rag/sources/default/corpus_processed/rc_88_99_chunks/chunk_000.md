## Synthesis of indoles: recent advances

## Dmitry I. Bugaenko, Alexander V. Karchava, Marina A. Yurovskaya

Department of Chemistry, Lomonosov Moscow State University Leninskie Gory 1, stroenie 3, 119991 Moscow, Russian Federation

Published data of the last 10 years concerning the development of new and upgrading of known approaches to indole synthesis are integrated and analyzed. Modern versions of the classical syntheses such as the Fischer synthesis, Nenitzescu synthesis, Ullmann reaction, Leimgruber-Batcho synthesis, Reissert synthesis, Bartoli reaction, Madelung synthesis and Cadogan-Sundberg reaction are considered. The presented new approaches include transformations of heterocycles, synthesis from o-alkynylanilines, reductive cyclization of nitrobenzene derivatives, synthesis with the use of arynes and catalysis by N-heterocyclic carbenes. The final Section summarizes original methods for the synthesis of indoles other than those listed above; they are classified in terms of the nature of the forming bond (C 7 C, C 7 N). Rarely used methods based on benzene ring construction in pyrrole derivatives are discussed separately. The bibliography includes 246 references.

Indole is one of the most abundant heterocycles occurring in natural products. Some indole derivatives are widely known to play an important role in the vital activity of humans and animals, namely, the amino acid tryptophan (1); the biogenic amines tryptamine (2) and serotonin (3); and the mammalian hormone melatonin responsible for psychiatric health (4). 1, 2

The diversity of useful properties of molecules containing the indole structural moiety constantly stimulates development of methods for their synthesis. In recent years, considerable efforts of chemical community have been directed not only towards the development of new synthetic routes to indole, but also towards advancement of the known classical approaches.

The interest in the problems of indole synthesis is indicated by a large number of literature reviews, both broad and specialized, that appeared in the last decade. The monograph of Gribble 4 published in 2016 addresses the synthesis and chemical properties of indoles ranging from natural compounds to pharmaceuticals and includes literature data from the mid-20th century up to recent times, but mainly up to 2014. Some general reviews cover publications up to 2011. 5 - 11 Most specialized reviews are devoted to the application of particular methods for indole ring formation, for example, to the use of isocyanates, 12 propargyl compounds, 13 azides 14 and isonitriles 15 or to particular indole structures. There are reviews considering the Bartoli reaction, 16 vicarious nucleophilic substitution, 17 Granberg tryptamine synthesis, 18 photochemical activation, 19 synthesis of fused indole derivatives 20 and indole dicarboxylic acids, 21 and indoles as potential medicinal agents for the treatment of neurodegenerative diseases. 22 The most recent reviews that appeared in the journal Chemistry of Heterocyclic Compounds in 2018 deal with modern catalytic methods for indole synthesis. 23 - 25

The absence of comprehensive reviews that would cover detailed information on the methods of indole synthesis of the last ten years, including both modern versions of classical methods and new synthetic approaches based on modern achievements of organic chemistry stimulated us to write this review.
