## 2.3. Ullmann reaction

N-Alkylated and N-arylated derivatives of methyl 1H-indole-3-carboxylate were synthesized for the first time by intramolecular Ullmann arylamination using CuI (Scheme 28). 59 This catalytic amination furnishes indole derivatives under mild reaction conditions in good to high yields. Under optimized conditions, this method was successfully used to prepare methyl 1H-indole-3-carboxylates with both aliphatic and aromatic substituents at the nitrogen atom (see Scheme 28). Attention is attracted by the possibility of preparing compounds with a chiral substituent at nitrogen, because the stereocentre of the chiral amine is not affected during the reaction.

This method was utilized for the synthesis of the simplest representative of N-isoprenylindole alkaloids, that is, methyl 1-(1,1-dimethylprop-2-en-1-yl)-1H-indole-3-carboxylate (40) (Scheme 29), isolated from Aporpium caryae basidiomycetes fungus. 60 This compound exhibits antifungal activity 61 and serves as the precursor for the synthesis of natural indole derivatives. Despite the seeming simplicity of structure 40, its synthesis is a non-trivial task because the direct introduction of the isoprenyl moiety into position 1 of indole is impossible.
