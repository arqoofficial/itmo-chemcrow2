The syntheses of functionalized indoles from o-aminophenylacetylenes are often associated with rearrangements and tandem or domino reactions. Indeed, in the case of NR1R2 o-alkynylanilines [R1 = Me, Et, Bn; R2 = C(O)R3, CO2R3, C(O)NHR3] and the PdCl2(MeCN)2 catalytic system, the functional substituent migrates to position 3 of the resulting indole. 135 A direct pathway to 2-aminoalkylindoles is the Cu-catalyzed three-component domino reaction of 2-ethynylaniline, secondary amines and aldehydes. 136 The direct ZnEt2-catalyzed anti-azacarboxylation of 2-alkynylanilines on treatment with CO2 yields indole-3-carboxylic acids. 137 The synthesis of indole-3-carboxamides from 2-ethynylanilines and isocyanates in the presence of a Rh catalyst proceeds as a cyclization-addition tandem reaction. 138, 139 An efficient synthesis of 3-aroylindoles from (o-dimethylaminophenyl)phenylacetylene with the Cu catalyst proceeds via activation of the C7H bond of the N-methyl group, intramolecular nucleophilic addition and tert-butyl hydroperoxide oxidation. 140

A selective synthesis of functionalized 3-allenyl-1H-indoles using tetra-n-butylammonium fluoride is a worthy alternative to the catalytic methods using noble metals (Scheme 102). 141 This approach, free from transition metal catalysts, is based on the efficiency of Bu4N+ and F7 ions in the reaction cascade including hydroamination of the carbon 7 carbon triple bond, elimination of the silyl protecting groups and site-selective sigmatropic [3,3]-aza Cope type rearrangement.
