## 4. Syntheses from o-alkynylanilines

The use of o-alkynylanilines is a part of a popular strategy for the synthesis of substituted indoles. o-Alkynylanilines are convenient substrates for annulation of a pyrrole ring to a benzene ring via intramolecular nucleophilic addition. 106 All methods of synthesis involving o-alkynylaniline derivatives can be split into several groups: (1) cyclization of ready o-alkynylaniline substrates under various conditions; (2) introduction of amine functions into the ortho-position of phenylacetylenes and their various uses; (3) utilization of the method to prepare functionalized indoles; (4) introduction of an ethynyl group into the ortho-position of the aniline molecule.

Using microwave radiation as an efficient activation method, one can obtain substituted indoles and azaindoles by the intramolecular cyclization of 2-alkynylanilines and alkynylpyridylamines (Scheme 75). 108 The cyclization proceeds with high yields and requires no transition metal-based catalysts.

The yields of reaction products virtually do not depend on the nature of substituent in the aromatic ring of alkynylamine. The highest yields were attained using 3-alkynyl4-aminopyridine. The yields of indoles and azaindoles sharply decrease if mere heating is used instead of microwave irradiation.

In the synthesis of 1,2-disubstituted indoles from 2-alkynylanilines and alkylboronic acids, copper acetate serves as the catalyst. 109

If the NH-acidity of the amine group is increased by introduction of an electron-withdrawing substituent to nitrogen, weak bases become suitable as catalysts for nucleophilic amination of the acetylene moiety. Thus, the cyclization of 2-ethynylN-sulfonylanilines to indoles smoothly proceeds in water in the presence of catalytic amounts of K2CO3 without transition metal catalysts to give products in high yields (Scheme 76, Table 7). 109 A possible reaction mechanism implies the K2CO3-induced formation of stabilized N-sulfonyl anion and nucleophilic addition of the anion to the triple bond.
