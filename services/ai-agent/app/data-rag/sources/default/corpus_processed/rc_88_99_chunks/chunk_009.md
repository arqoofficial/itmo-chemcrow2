On the basis of calculations, the authors proposed a mechanism of intramolecular hydroamination of a model system, 2-(prop-1-ynyl)aniline, using ZnX2, which gives 2-methylindole. 110 Structural optimization identifies four stable intermediates: N-coordinated complex A, p-coordinated complex B, N- and p-coordinated complex C and s-complex D. The calculations confirm that complex C is the most stable one. Since the potential energy difference between complexes B and C is 16.8 kca mol 7 1, the reaction can proceed in the following way: ZnX2 reacts with 2-alkynylaniline to give complex C, which occurs in equilibrium with complex B, the latter cyclizes to afford s-complex D, which is protodemetallated to yield indole and ZnX2 (Scheme 79).
