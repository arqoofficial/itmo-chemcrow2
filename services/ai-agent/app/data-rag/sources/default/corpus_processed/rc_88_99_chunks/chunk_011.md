The yields of reaction products virtually do not depend on the nature of substituent in the aromatic ring of alkynylamine. The highest yields were attained using 3-alkynyl4-aminopyridine. The yields of indoles and azaindoles sharply decrease if mere heating is used instead of microwave irradiation.

In the synthesis of 1,2-disubstituted indoles from 2-alkynylanilines and alkylboronic acids, copper acetate serves as the catalyst. 109

If the NH-acidity of the amine group is increased by introduction of an electron-withdrawing substituent to nitrogen, weak bases become suitable as catalysts for nucleophilic amination of the acetylene moiety. Thus, the cyclization of 2-ethynyl N-sulfonylanilines to indoles smoothly proceeds in water in the presence of catalytic amounts of K2CO3 without transition metal catalysts to give products in high yields (Scheme 76, Table 7). 109 A possible reaction mechanism implies the K2CO3-induced formation of stabilized N-sulfonyl anion and nucleophilic addition of the anion to the triple bond.
