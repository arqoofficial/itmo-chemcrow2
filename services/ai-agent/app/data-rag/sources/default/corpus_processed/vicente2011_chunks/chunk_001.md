Formation of 3-indolylpalladium intermediates similar to 11 has been further exploited in order to introduce other valuable functionalities. 14 In this sense, de Lera reported very recently the preparation of alkenyl-substituted indoles combining a palladium-catalyzed cyclization with an oxidative Heck-coupling. 15 Thus, in the presence of acrylate 15 , aniline 14 could be converted into 3alkenylindole 18 in good yield under aerobic reaction conditions using PdCl2 as catalyst (Scheme 4). A plausible mechanism could start with an aminopalladation, followed by an alkene insertion to generate intermediate 17 . Indole 18 was obtained after b-hydride elimination. The presence of O2 likely enabled the regeneration of an active PdX2 species through oxidation. Interestingly, b-hydride elimination in intermediates such as 17 could be avoided using LiBr, affording the corresponding 3-alkylindoles. 16
