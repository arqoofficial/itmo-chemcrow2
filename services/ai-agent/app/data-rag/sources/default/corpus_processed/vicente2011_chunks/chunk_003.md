A novel route to indole synthesis using o-dihaloarenes and imines was devised by Barluenga. 36 In this methodology an a-deprotonation of the imine set the stage to allow two consecutive palladium-catalyzed cross-coupling processes. Thus, under basic conditions, imine 71 was a-arylated with unsymmetric dihaloarene 70 to yield intermediate imine 72, which tautomerized to the corresponding enamine 73. A subsequent BuchwaldHartwig amination gave rise to 2,3-disubstituted indole 74 in good yield (Scheme 17). Remarkably, the regioselectivity of the overall transformation could be controlled according to the preference of the oxidative addition to the C-Br- over the C-Cl-bond.

This reaction was found to proceed regioselectively and with the same efficiency using o-chloroaryl sulfonates, which could be prepared from readily available inexpensive phenols. 37 Moreover, a convenient 'on water' microwave-promoted protocol of this transformation was developed as well. 38 Similarly, Barluenga found that indoles could be built in a modular three-component cascade reaction employing simple starting materials such as bromoalkenes, primary amines and o-dihaloarenes. 36 In this manner, indole 79 was efficiently prepared from b-bromostyrene (59), aniline (75) and 1,2-dichlorobenzene (76) (Scheme 18).
